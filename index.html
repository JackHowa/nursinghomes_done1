Some State Nursing Homes Report Fewer Than 30 Minutes Per Resident

By Jack Howard, KBIA
Published April 8, 2015 (exp)

In 2014, the Center for Public Integrity compiled national Medicare data on nursing home care. Many nursing homes reported fewer than half an hour per patient per day. These nursing facilities are in purple. Marilyn Rantz, an executive director at Tiger Place, a Columbia, Mo. facility, said some nursing homes have struggled with the shift to new reporting technology. Last year, there were new requirements to use electronic recording data. The Improving Medicare Post-Acute Care Transformation Act of 2014, or Impact Act, was signed into law by President Obama in October 2014. The proposed pilot recording programs requires nationwide reporting by the end of 2016. The Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services announced in April a new policy on electronic staffing data submissions adopted by October 1.

Source: Center for Public Integrity
